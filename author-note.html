Author Notes

The whispers behind the words.

Writing is not therapy. It’s self-harm with punctuation. If you feel seen in these pages, it’s because you’re bleeding too. These aren’t stories—they’re screams wrapped in metaphors.

I don’t write to be understood. I write because the silence kills more than any scream ever could. To everyone who reads my chaos and still comes back—I see you. I feel you. I break with you.

Every ending hurts because somewhere, a beginning is still begging to exist. And love? Love doesn’t save you. It just makes the wreck look poetic.

Unstable Bonus Thoughts

  • “Readers ask for sequels. My demons write them uninvited.”
  • “Art is what happens when reality finally breaks you.”
  • “I don’t edit my work. Pain doesn’t come with revisions.”
